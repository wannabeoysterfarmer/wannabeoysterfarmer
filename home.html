Railroad Photography

My art journey began with photographing the railroad landscape. In college, I had

the fortune of being on a campus with the CSX mainline running through it. Railway

photography is fast-paced and offers an infinite array of possibilities, ranging

from landscapes, to pan shots, to long exposures, to night trails. Exploring the

world of railways from behind the viewfinder never ceases to amaze me.

 

 

Landscapes & Night Photography

Outside of exploring railroads, I enjoy shooting natural landscapes as well as night-

scapes. Although shots during daylight and golden hour are welcome, I've recently

taken on a greater interest in photographing at night, playing with hues and unique

weather conditions to portray a dreamy, futuristic vibe reminiscent of cyberpunk

and fantasy.

 

 

Drone Photography

The world often takes on a whole new perspective when viewed from above!

So many new angles to try out and so many places to explore. I use a DJI

Mavic Mini and shoot both photo and video. This photo portrays a westbound

freight train tackling the grade at Brookfield, WI, passing the suburbs

on a snow-covered day.

 

 

Aerial Videography

Videography (especially aerial) captured my interest after I purchased a drone. In

this video, we see six of Canadian Pacific's cherry-red locomotives hauling a heavy

westbound freight train through the sleepy town of Brookfield, WI on a winter afternoon.

Shot with my DJI Mavic Mini drone. Music: Avicii - Waiting for Love // Date: 12/26/2020

 

 

Aerial Videography

On a snow-covered evening in Madison, WI, we see the regular evening freight train T5

pull through town, just minutes away from arriving at their terminal on the east side

of the city. Music: Alan Walker - Lost Control (Instrumental) // Winter 2020.

 

 

Color Illustration - "Astro Kid"

After taking Scott Harris's illustration course on Udemy, I brushed up on my drawing

skills and created a few pieces while quarantined during the COVID-19 pandemic.

"Astro Kid" is a piece about hypothetical space travel in a dreamy, futuristic world.

Steven Chen "Choo-Choo"

I'm an explorer and adventurist at heart, with interests in digital media ranging from photography

to videography to illustration.

 

Most of what I know is self-taught through trial-and-error and supplemented through online learning.

Some of my recent recognitions include being selected as a 2020 Docent Scholarship recipient by the

Center for Railroad Photography & Art, and as the Grand Prize and Third Place Winner of the 2020

John E. Gruber Creative Photography Awards contest (international).

 

With my academic background in chemistry, economics, and computer science, I tend to approach

my work through a highly precise and detailed lens. I graduated from Emory University in 2020 and

am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Computer Science with the University of Pennsylvania.
